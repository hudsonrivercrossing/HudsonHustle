# V2.1 Design Critique And Reference

## Summary

`v2.1` should use `ideago.cc` as a strong directional reference for shell design, especially:
- typography confidence
- layout rhythm
- whitespace discipline
- editorial hierarchy
- premium mood through restraint

The goal is not resemblance. The goal is to make Hudson Hustle feel more singular and more designed without weakening gameplay clarity.

## Adopt
- oversized but disciplined heading hierarchy
- stronger section rhythm
- more whitespace as structure
- typography as a primary brand layer
- fewer competing visual treatments
- premium feeling through proportion and restraint, not added ornament

## Adapt
- apply editorial energy mainly to:
  - setup
  - lobby
  - shell framing
  - status
  - panels
  - cards
- keep gameplay controls operational and fast to scan
- let refined brand tone shape shell surfaces, not core game-state readability
- allow limited influence on the map only where it improves composure, not where it risks legibility

## Avoid
- turning the app into a portfolio site
- lowering contrast in the name of elegance
- over-minimalizing gameplay-critical UI
- letting decorative subtlety obscure route ownership or turn state
- copying the surface look of `ideago.cc` instead of extracting its discipline

## Critique Of Current Hudson Hustle UI
- The board is stronger than the shell. The map already feels like Hudson Hustle; the surrounding product shell is still more prototype-like.
- Typography is functional but not yet brand-defining.
- Panels, banners, and rails are useful, but too many areas sit at similar visual weight.
- The interface lacks a strong editorial rhythm. It reads as competent assembly more than authored composition.
- The current tactile/transit-nostalgia direction is worth preserving; the issue is not the concept, but the strength and coherence of its execution.

## V2.1 Design Principles
1. Typography should become the primary shell identity layer.
2. The board remains the visual anchor of the product.
3. Favor fewer, stronger layout moves over many medium-strength treatments.
4. Separate shell mood from gameplay clarity; never trade readability for refinement.
5. Let whitespace and alignment do more work than borders and extra chrome.
6. Treat status, banner, chip, and card language as one family.
7. Keep local play and multiplayer in one visual family, but allow them different pacing and tone.
8. Only allow editorial influence onto the map where it improves composure without harming legibility.
